Ethiopia noun /iːθiˈəʊpiə/

Ethiopia is one of the oldest nations in the world, with a history that stretches back thousands of years. Often referred to as the birthplace of humanity, the country is home to some of the earliest human fossils ever discovered, including "Lucy," a 3.2-million-year-old hominid found in the Afar region. Ancient civilizations began to form in northern Ethiopia as early as the 10th century BCE, starting with the Kingdom of Dʿmt.

This early kingdom set the stage for the rise of the Kingdom of Aksum, which became one of the most powerful empires of the ancient world. Centered in what is now northern Ethiopia and Eritrea, Aksum was known for its architectural marvels such as towering obelisks, its own written script called Ge'ez, and its role as a major trading hub connecting Africa, the Middle East, and Asia.

Ethiopia is known for its strong cultural identity and long-standing sovereignty. It resisted colonization, most notably defeating Italian forces at the Battle of Adwa in 1896. The country is home to a rich cultural heritage, ancient architecture, and archaeological sites. In recent years, Ethiopia has been undergoing rapid modernization, with major infrastructure projects, urban development, and growing global influence.

Events

Battle of Adwa

The Battle of Adwa (Amharic: የዐድዋ ጦርነት; Tigrinya: ውግእ ዓድዋ; Italian: battaglia di Adua, also spelled Adowa) was the climactic battle of the First Italo-Ethiopian War. The Ethiopian army defeated an invading Italian and Eritrean force led by Oreste Baratieri on March 1, 1896, near the town of Adwa.

Battle of Dogali

Italy sought to establish a colonial empire after unification in 1861, which led to conflict with Ethiopia over Eritrea. The 1894 Treaty of Wuchale, which Italy claimed granted them a protectorate over Ethiopia, was disputed by King Menelik II. This led to the First Italo-Ethiopian War in 1895.

Treasures

Lalibela

Lalibela is a town in the Amhara region of Ethiopia, famous for its rock-hewn churches, which are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. These 11 medieval monolithic churches were carved out of rock in the 12th century and are considered one of Ethiopia's greatest architectural achievements.

Axum Obelisks

The Axum Obelisks are ancient monolithic structures located in the city of Axum, Ethiopia. They are among the most significant archaeological sites in Ethiopia and are believed to date back to the 4th century AD, serving as markers for royal graves.
