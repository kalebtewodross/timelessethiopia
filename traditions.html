Traditions of Ethiopia

Discover the cultural expressions, culinary customs, and everyday traditions that make Ethiopia unique.

Eskista

Eskista is a traditional Ethiopian dance characterized by rhythmic shoulder movements and expressive gestures.

Coffee

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is a social ritual involving roasting, brewing, and serving coffee, symbolizing hospitality and friendship.

Gursha

Gursha is the act of feeding someone else with your hand as a sign of affection, love, and community bonding.

Market Culture

Weekly markets are lively community hubs where people trade goods and socialize, each with unique customs and rhythms.

Enkutatash

Celebrated on September 11, this festival marks the Ethiopian New Year with songs, flowers, and renewal traditions.

Injera Preparation

Preparing injera from teff flour is a skill passed down through generations, central to Ethiopian cuisine and communal meals.
